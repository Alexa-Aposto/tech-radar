## [RoMem: Continuous Phase Rotation for Temporal Knowledge Graphs and Agentic Memory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11544v1) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers have introduced RoMem, a novel temporal knowledge graph module that uses continuous phase rotation and a Semantic Speed Gate to manage the volatility of relations in structured memory. This approach allows obsolete facts to be rotated out of phase without deletion, enabling temporally correct facts to naturally outrank contradictions. This innovation addresses a key limitation in current AI agent memory systems, which often struggle to differentiate between evolving and persistent knowledge. By providing a more effective and efficient structured memory, RoMem could significantly enhance the capabilities of long-lived AI systems and autonomous agents. RoMem employs a Semantic Speed Gate to learn the volatility of relations, mapping text embeddings to a volatility score, and utilizes continuous phase rotation to achieve 'geometric shadowing'. This method has demonstrated state-of-the-art results on temporal knowledge graph completion and improved performance in agentic memory tasks.

**Background**: Existing temporal knowledge graph approaches often treat time as discrete metadata, leading to issues with managing outdated or evolving information. This can involve burying old knowledge, overwriting facts, or requiring costly LLM calls. RoMem proposes a geometric approach to time, inspired by cognitive neuroscience, where time is encoded as continuous geometric trajectories.

## [MISE: Reinforcement Learning with Hindsight Self-Evaluation for LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11611v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers have introduced Mutual Information Self-Evaluation (MISE), a novel reinforcement learning paradigm that addresses sparse reward challenges for LLM-based agents by using hindsight generative self-evaluation as dense reward signals. This method is theoretically grounded in mutual information and KL divergence, and it calibrates these internal rewards against environmental feedback. This development is significant because it provides a method for LLM agents to learn more effectively in complex environments where external rewards are infrequent. This could lead to more capable autonomous AI agents that can operate and make decisions within systems like Kubernetes. MISE enables autonomous learning from dense internal rewards that supplement sparse extrinsic signals, and it has been shown to enable open-source LLMs of around 7B parameters to achieve performance comparable to GPT-4o without expert supervision. The theoretical foundation proves that using hindsight self-evaluation rewards is equivalent to minimizing an objective combining mutual information with a KL divergence term between the policy and a proxy reward policy.

**Background**: Reinforcement learning (RL) agents learn by interacting with an environment and receiving rewards. However, in many complex scenarios, rewards are sparse, meaning the agent only receives feedback after a long sequence of actions, making learning inefficient. Hindsight experience replay is a technique used to address sparse rewards by treating failed attempts as successful ones for different goals.
