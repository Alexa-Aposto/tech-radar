This approach allows obsolete facts to be rotated out of phase, enabling temporally correct facts to naturally outrank contradictions without deletion, achieving state-of-the-art results on temporal knowledge graph completion benchmarks. This innovation addresses a critical limitation in current temporal knowledge representation by moving beyond discrete time metadata and costly LLM calls. It enables AI agents and long-lived systems to more effectively distinguish between persistent and evolving information, leading to improved temporal reasoning and memory management. RoMem employs a Semantic Speed Gate to learn relation volatility and uses continuous phase rotation to implement geometric shadowing, where outdated information is phased out in vector space. This method reportedly achieves state-of-the-art performance on temporal knowledge graph completion and improves temporal reasoning accuracy in agentic memory tasks.
